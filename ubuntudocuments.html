UbuntuPath – Volunteer Code of Conduct

UbuntuPath volunteers serve communities with integrity, dignity, and respect. As representatives of a growing movement, volunteers are expected to uphold the values of unity, honesty, and lawful civic engagement at all times.

1. Purpose of Volunteering

Volunteering with UbuntuPath is voluntary and unpaid. Volunteers support community engagement, membership recruitment, signature collection, fundraising awareness, and outreach initiatives.

2. Expected Conduct

  • Act honestly, respectfully, and lawfully at all times
  • Treat all people equally regardless of race, gender, religion, or political belief
  • Promote UbuntuPath truthfully without intimidation, coercion, or misinformation
  • Never promise jobs, money, benefits, or favours in exchange for support

3. Donations & Fundraising

  • Volunteers may not retain cash or donations under any circumstances
  • All contributions must be directed to official UbuntuPath banking channels
  • No receipts may be issued unless formally authorised by UbuntuPath

4. Representation & Communication

  • Volunteers are not official spokespeople unless appointed in writing
  • No media statements may be made on behalf of UbuntuPath without approval
  • The UbuntuPath name, logo, or materials may not be used for personal gain

5. Signatures & Personal Information

  • Signatures must be collected freely and voluntarily
  • No inducements, pressure, or misrepresentation is permitted
  • Signatures may not be collected from minors
  • Personal information must be respected and kept confidential

6. Compliance & Discipline

Failure to comply with this Code of Conduct may result in suspension or removal from volunteer activities. UbuntuPath reserves the right to revoke volunteer status at its discretion.

By volunteering with UbuntuPath, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agreed to abide by this Code of Conduct.
